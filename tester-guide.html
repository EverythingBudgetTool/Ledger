What I'm hoping to learn: not just "does it work," but where it's confusing, where you had to guess, and anything that made you stop and think "wait, what?" There are no wrong notes — a UI that only makes sense to the person who built it is a real bug.
Two ways to try it — pick either (or both):

Option A — Use the sample data. I've included a demo backup file (the-ledger-demo-backup.json) full of a made-up family's finances. Import it and every screen fills with realistic data instantly — nothing of yours involved. Easiest way to see the whole app working, and safe to delete, edit, and experiment freely.

Option B — Use your own data. If you already keep a budget you can export, importing your own numbers is the best way to catch real bugs — you'll instantly notice when something looks wrong, because you know what right looks like. Your data stays entirely on your own device. If you go this route, treat your backup file as sensitive, like any financial document.

Not sure? Start with Option A — it takes ten seconds and you can always clear it and import your own later.
